## 1. Critical Context: Current Federal Landscape

The **One Big Beautiful Bill Act** (P.L. 119-21), signed July 4, 2025, terminated most Inflation Reduction Act clean vehicle credits. Key takeaway:

- **Sections 30D, 25E, and 45W credits are no longer available** for vehicles acquired after **September 30, 2025**.
- The **Section 30C charger credit** survives through **June 30, 2026**.
- Vehicles acquired on or before Sept 30, 2025 (via binding written contract + payment) can still be claimed on 2025 tax returns filed in 2026.

### Federal Credit Status Summary

| Credit | Status (March 2026) | Max Amount | Deadline |
|--------|---------------------|------------|----------|
| 30D (New EV) | Terminated | $7,500 | Acquired by Sept 30, 2025 |
| 25E (Used EV) | Terminated | $4,000 | Acquired by Sept 30, 2025 |
| 45W (Commercial EV) | Terminated | $7,500 / $40,000 | Acquired by Sept 30, 2025 |
| 30C (Charger Install) | **Still Active** | $1,000 (individual) / $100,000 (business) | Placed in service by June 30, 2026 |

## 2. Federal Incentives

### Section 30D: New Clean Vehicle Credit

**Status: TERMINATED** for vehicles acquired after September 30, 2025.

**Credit Amount:** Up to **$7,500**, split into two components:
- **$3,750** if the vehicle meets **critical mineral sourcing** requirements
- **$3,750** if the vehicle meets **battery component** requirements

A vehicle can qualify for $0, $3,750, or $7,500 depending on compliance.

**MSRP Caps:**
- Sedans/coupes/hatchbacks: **$55,000**
- Vans/SUVs/pickup trucks: **$80,000**
- Based on MSRP (sticker price), not negotiated price

**Income Limits (Modified Adjusted Gross Income):**

| Filing Status | MAGI Limit |
|---------------|------------|
| Married Filing Jointly | $300,000 |
| Head of Household | $225,000 |
| Single / All Others | $150,000 |

- **Hard cliff**: exceeding by $1 disqualifies entirely (no phase-out)
- Can use MAGI from either purchase year or prior year (whichever is lower)

**Critical Minerals & Battery Component Requirements:**
- Critical minerals: specified % must be extracted/processed in the US or free trade agreement countries, or recycled in North America (50% in 2024, 60% in 2025)
- Battery components: specified % must be manufactured/assembled in North America (60% in 2024-2025)
- **FEOC exclusion**: vehicles with battery components from China, Russia, North Korea, or Iran are disqualified (starting 2024 for components, 2025 for minerals)

**Vehicle Requirements:**
- BEVs, PHEVs (min 7 kWh battery), or FCEVs
- Final assembly in North America
- Made by a qualified manufacturer

**How to Claim:**
1. **Point-of-sale transfer**: dealer applies credit as price reduction at time of purchase (dealer must be IRS-registered)
2. **Tax return filing**: claim on Form 8936 (credit is nonrefundable -- only reduces tax to zero, no refund beyond that, no carry-forward)

---

### Section 25E: Used Clean Vehicle Credit

**Status: TERMINATED** for vehicles acquired after September 30, 2025.

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Credit amount | **30% of sale price**, up to **$4,000** max |
| Price cap | Sale price must be **$25,000 or less** |
| Income limits | $75,000 single / $112,500 HOH / $150,000 MFJ |
| Seller | Must buy from a **licensed dealer** (no private sales) |
| First transfer only | Must be first resale after 8/16/2022 |
| Model year | At least **2 years older** than calendar year of purchase |
| Frequency | One credit per taxpayer every **3 years** |
| Nonrefundable | Cannot exceed tax liability; no carry-forward |

**Key differences from 30D:**
- No assembly location requirement
- No critical minerals/battery component requirements
- No FEOC restrictions
- Lower income thresholds and credit cap

---

### Section 45W: Commercial Clean Vehicle Credit

**Status: TERMINATED** for vehicles acquired after September 30, 2025.

**Credit Amount** (lesser of):
1. **30% of vehicle cost** (15% for PHEVs)
2. Incremental cost over comparable ICE vehicle
3. Cap: **$7,500** (under 14,000 lbs GVWR) or **$40,000** (14,000+ lbs)

**Key Advantages Over 30D:**
- No MSRP cap
- No income limit
- No critical minerals/battery component requirements
- No FEOC restrictions
- No North American assembly requirement
- Credit **can be carried forward** as a general business credit

**Eligibility:** Vehicle must be used in a trade or business (subject to depreciation). Available to businesses, tax-exempt orgs, and government entities.

---

### Section 30C: EV Charger Installation Credit (STILL ACTIVE)

**Expires: June 30, 2026**

**Individuals:** 30% of cost, up to **$1,000 per charging port**

**Businesses:**
- Base: 6% of cost, up to $100,000 per item
- With prevailing wage/apprenticeship: 30% of cost, up to $100,000 per item

**Location Requirement:** Must be installed in a low-income community census tract or non-urban (rural) census tract. This significantly limits eligibility.

---

### NEVI (National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure) Program

- $5 billion from the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (FY2022-2026)
- FY2026 apportionment: $885 million to states
- Grant program to states for public charging infrastructure (not a direct consumer incentive)
- ~384 charging ports built as of early 2026; 21+ states opening new funding in Q1 2026

## 3. State Incentives

With federal credits expired, state programs are now the primary source of purchase incentives.

### California

**Clean Cars 4 All (CC4A):**
- Up to **$12,000** toward new or used BEV/PHEV/FCEV + **$2,000** for charging equipment (total **$14,000**)
- Income limit: household at or below 300% Federal Poverty Level (~$93,600 for family of 4)
- Must be in a participating air district
- Requires scrapping an older, high-emitting vehicle

**Driving Clean Assistance Program (DCAP):**
- Assists income-qualified residents with EV purchase/lease
- Income threshold: at or below 300% FPL
- Can combine with CC4A in some districts

*Note: The original CVRP (Clean Vehicle Rebate Project) closed permanently November 8, 2023.*

### Colorado

**State EV Tax Credit:**
- **2025:** $3,500 for new EVs (MSRP up to $80K) + additional $2,500 for EVs under $35K MSRP (total $6,000)
- **2026:** Standard credit drops to **$750**; the $2,500 under-$35K bonus remains

**Vehicle Exchange Colorado (VXC):**
- $9,000 (new BEV/PHEV) or $6,000 (used BEV/PHEV)
- Income-qualified; requires trading in older vehicle
- Point-of-sale rebate at dealership
- Combined with state tax credit: up to **$15,000** total

### New York

**Drive Clean Rebate (NYSERDA):**
- Up to **$2,000** off a new EV at point of sale
- No income limit for standard rebate
- Applied by participating dealers automatically
- Program received additional $30 million in funding

### New Jersey

**Charge Up New Jersey:**
- **$4,000** total ($1,500 base + $2,500 Charge Up+ for income-qualifying applicants)
- $250 rebate for Level 2 home charger
- Point-of-sale rebate at dealership

*Note: Sales tax exemption expired. Full 6.625% sales tax now applies to ZEVs sold on or after July 1, 2025.*

### Massachusetts

**MOR-EV:**
- **$3,500** standard rebate for new BEVs (MSRP up to $55K)
- **$1,500** additional MOR-EV+ adder for income-qualifying applicants
- **$1,000** trade-in incentive
- **Total possible: $6,000** for income-qualified with trade-in
- Used EVs: $3,500 (purchase price up to $40K, income-qualified)

### Connecticut

**CHEAPR (as of October 1, 2025):**

*Standard:* New BEV $1,000 / New PHEV $500

*Rebate+ (income-qualified):*
- New BEV: **$4,000** / New PHEV: $2,000
- Used BEV: **$5,000** / Used PHEV: $3,000

MSRP cap: $50,000 for new vehicles.

### Illinois

**EV Rebate Program (CEJA):**
- **$4,000** for low-income applicants ($2,000 base + $2,000 adder)
- **$2,000** for all others
- Income ceiling: 500% FPL (~$160,750 for family of 4)
- Base purchase price max $80,000; must buy from IL licensed dealer
- Application deadline: May 31, 2026
- Drops to $2,000 for vehicles purchased July 1, 2026 - June 30, 2027

### Oregon

**Status: SUSPENDED** (standard rebate suspended Sept 9, 2025; Charge Ahead suspended Dec 5, 2025 due to depleted funding)
- Was up to $7,500 (new) / $5,000 (used) for income-qualified buyers
- No new funding announced for 2026

### Maryland

**Excise Tax Credit:**
- Up to $3,000 for new zero-emission vehicles (purchase price up to $50K)
- **FY26 funding depleted** -- applications on hold

### Other Notable States

| State | Program | Amount | Notes |
|-------|---------|--------|-------|
| Maine | State rebate | $2,000-$7,500 (income-tiered) | Suspended except for low-income |
| Vermont | State incentive | Varies | Fully reserved/closed |

- Source: [CarsDirect](https://www.carsdirect.com/deals-articles/hyundai-competitive-owner-coupon-explained)

### Dealer-Side Incentives (Hidden from Consumers)

**Dealer Holdback:**
- A % of MSRP the manufacturer pays back to the dealer (usually quarterly)
- Ford/GM/Stellantis: ~3% of MSRP
- Toyota/Honda: ~2% of MSRP
- On a $50,000 vehicle with 3% holdback = $1,500 guaranteed dealer profit
- Dealers can sell at or below invoice and still profit

**Dealer Cash:**
- Manufacturer-to-dealer bonuses for moving specific models ($500-$5,000+ per vehicle)
- Not advertised to consumers; dealer may or may not pass savings through
- "Stair-step" programs: escalating bonuses for hitting volume thresholds (e.g., sell 50 = $500/unit, sell 75 = $1,000/unit)

**Dealer Choice Bonus Cash (New for 2025-2026):**
- A financing incentive administered by the manufacturer's captive finance arm
- Example: 2026 Hyundai IONIQ 5 offers up to **$10,000** Dealer Choice Bonus Cash through Hyundai Motor Finance
- **Key catch:** Requires financing at a dealer-set rate (often 5.99-6.99% APR for 72 months)
- The $10,000 is applied as a down payment — NOT a cash rebate
- Often **mutually exclusive** with 0% APR offers (e.g., IONIQ 5 also offers 0% APR + $5,000 as an alternative)
- **Consumer tip:** For buyers with top-tier credit, the 0% APR + lower bonus cash may save MORE in total cost than the large headline Dealer Choice number at higher rates
- Dealers control the final rate markup, so always compare total cost of ownership
- Source: [CarsDirect](https://www.carsdirect.com/deals-articles/2026-hyundai-ioniq-5-10-000-rebate-isn-t-what-you-think)

**True dealer cost** = Invoice - Holdback - Dealer Cash - Volume Bonuses

Average industry incentive spending as of mid-2025: **7.3% of average transaction price**.

## 5. Lease-Specific Incentives & the EV Lease Loophole

### How Manufacturers Subsidize Leases

- **Inflated residual values**: higher projected end-of-lease value = lower monthly payments
- **Reduced money factors**: subsidized lease interest rate
- **Lease-specific bonus cash**: sometimes offered in addition to purchase rebates
- All controlled by captive finance arms

### The Commercial Credit "Loophole" (Now Expired)

When leasing an EV, the **leasing company** (not the consumer) is the purchaser. This meant:
- The leasing company claimed the **Section 45W Commercial Clean Vehicle Credit**
- Section 45W had **no income limits**, **no MSRP caps**, **no sourcing requirements**, **no FEOC restrictions**
- **Any EV** could qualify for the full $7,500 -- including foreign-assembled vehicles
- Most manufacturers passed the credit through as lower monthly payments
- EV lease rates surged from ~15% (2022) to **67% (March 2025)** because of this

**This loophole closed September 30, 2025.** Post-expiration, EV lease payments are expected to increase unless manufacturers step in with additional subsidies.

## 6. How to Qualify & Claim

### Income Verification

**MAGI Definition for the EV credit:**
- MAGI = AGI + amounts excluded under IRC Sections 911, 931, 933 (foreign earned income, Guam/Samoa/CNMI/Puerto Rico income)
- **For most domestic taxpayers: MAGI = AGI** (Line 11 of Form 1040)

**Which year's income:**
- Qualify if MAGI is below the limit for **either** the purchase year **or** the prior year
- Taxpayer-friendly: if one year is over and the other is under, you still qualify

### VIN Lookup & Qualified Vehicle Lists

- **FuelEconomy.gov**: Primary resource listing qualifying vehicles and credit amounts
- **AFDC VIN Decoder**: Verifies final assembly location
- **NHTSA VIN Decoder**: vpic.nhtsa.dot.gov
- **Always check by specific VIN before purchasing** -- same model can have different assembly locations or battery configs across trims

### Point-of-Sale Transfer Mechanism

- Available starting January 2024 for both new (30D) and used (25E) credits
- Dealer must be registered with IRS Energy Credits Online portal
- Provides immediate price reduction instead of waiting for tax return
- Election is **irrevocable** once made
- If you later don't qualify (income too high), **IRS recaptures the credit** on your tax return

### Common Pitfalls and Disqualifications

1. **Exceeding income limits**: Hard cliff, no phase-out. $1 over = full disqualification. If transferred at point of sale, IRS recaptures.
2. **Vehicle not on qualified list**: Don't assume -- check by VIN on fueleconomy.gov.
3. **MSRP vs purchase price confusion**: MSRP cap is based on sticker price, not negotiated price. A $56K MSRP car is disqualified even if bought for $50K.
4. **Dealer not registered**: Not all dealers registered for point-of-sale transfers.
5. **Used EV private sale**: Section 25E requires purchase from a licensed dealer.
6. **Used EV "first transfer" rule**: Credit only applies to first resale after 8/16/2022.
7. **Tax liability too low**: Credit is nonrefundable. If tax liability < credit, you lose the difference permanently (no carry-forward for individuals).
8. **Vehicle returned within 30 days**: Cannot claim credit; transferred amount recaptured.
9. **Timing**: "Placed in service" = delivery date, not contract/deposit date.

### Documentation to Keep (3+ years)

- VIN
- Purchase agreement / bill of sale
- Window sticker (Monroney label) showing MSRP and assembly location
- Seller/dealer time-of-sale report
- Transfer election paperwork (if applicable)
- Form 8936 (must be filed even if credit was transferred at point of sale)

## 8. Non-Monetary Incentives

### HOV Lane Access

**Federal authorization expired September 30, 2025.** States that previously allowed single-occupant EV access (CA, AZ, CO, FL, GA, HI, MD, NJ, NY, NC, UT, VA) now enforce standard occupancy rules. Legislation to reinstate (HOV Lane Exemption Reauthorization Act) has been introduced but not enacted.

### EV Registration Fees (Negative Incentive)

**39 states charge additional EV registration fees** ($50-$400/year) to replace lost gas tax revenue. Exception: DC offers reduced $36 fee for first 2 years.

### Other

- Some municipalities offer free/discounted public charging
- Certain toll road discounts in select jurisdictions
- Workplace charging mandates in some states (e.g., CA building codes)

## 9. Stacking: How Incentives Combine

All incentive types are generally **independent and stackable**:

- Manufacturer rebates do not affect MSRP (which determines federal credit eligibility)
- Federal, state, and utility incentives stack with each other
- Affinity discounts stack with manufacturer rebates
- Dealer discounts stack with everything

**Example (pre-Sept 2025 purchase in Colorado):**

| Source | Amount |
|--------|--------|
| Federal 30D credit | $7,500 |
| Colorado state tax credit | $3,500-$6,000 |
| Colorado VXC (income-qualified) | $6,000-$9,000 |
| Manufacturer rebate | $2,000-$10,000 |
| Affinity discount | $500-$1,000 |
| Dealer negotiation | Varies |
| **Total potential** | **$19,500-$33,500+** |

**Current stacking (March 2026, no federal credits):**

| Source | Amount |
|--------|--------|
| State rebate/credit (varies) | $750-$14,000 |
| Utility rebate | $50-$4,200 |
| Manufacturer rebate | $500-$12,750 |
| Affinity discount | $500-$1,500 |
| 30C charger credit | Up to $1,000 |
| **Total potential** | **Varies widely by state and vehicle** |
