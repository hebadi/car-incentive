## 1. Executive Summary

**IncentiveDrive** is an automotive lead generation platform that differentiates through deep incentive intelligence. While existing platforms (AutoTrader, CarGurus, Cars.com, TrueCar) focus on inventory search and price comparison, IncentiveDrive helps consumers discover the full savings stack -- federal, state, manufacturer, dealer, utility, and affinity incentives -- and connects them with dealerships as high-intent, pre-qualified leads.

### The Opportunity

- The federal EV tax credit expired September 30, 2025, creating massive consumer confusion about remaining incentives
- State programs now vary wildly ($0 to $14,000+), and manufacturer incentives change monthly
- Dealers spend an average of **$543,539/year** on advertising, with **$283 average CPL** across all sources
- **30% of third-party leads are estimated to be fraudulent** -- dealers are desperate for higher-quality leads
- No existing platform specializes in incentive-aware lead generation

## 2. Value Proposition

### For Consumers

**Problem:** Incentive complexity is overwhelming. A buyer in Colorado could stack state credits ($3,250), manufacturer rebates ($2,000-$12,750), utility rebates ($50-$4,200), affinity discounts ($500-$1,500), and the remaining 30C charger credit ($1,000) -- but no single resource shows all of this personalized to their situation.

**Solution:** IncentiveDrive provides a personalized incentive calculator that:
- Takes consumer inputs (zip code, income range, vehicle interest, military/educator status, current vehicle)
- Returns a comprehensive savings estimate across all incentive layers
- Shows which vehicles maximize total savings
- Connects consumers to local dealers with pre-calculated incentive packages

### For Dealers

**Problem:** Dealers pay $283/lead on average with 5-8% close rates. Third-party leads are often low-intent, duplicated, and 30% fraudulent.

**Solution:** IncentiveDrive delivers leads that are:
- **Higher intent** -- consumers who have already calculated their personalized savings are further down the funnel
- **Pre-qualified** -- income range, vehicle preferences, and eligibility confirmed upfront
- **Incentive-informed** -- dealer knows exactly which incentives apply, enabling faster deal structuring
- **Exclusive** -- leads are sold to a maximum of 3 dealers (vs. unlimited distribution on some platforms)

### Competitive Moat

1. **Incentive data engine** -- Continuously updated database of 200+ incentive programs across federal, state, local, manufacturer, dealer, and utility layers
2. **Personalization** -- No competitor offers personalized, stacked incentive calculations
3. **Higher conversion rates** -- Incentive-aware leads convert at higher rates because the savings are already quantified
4. **Network effects** -- More dealer partners = better geographic coverage = more consumer trust = more leads

## 3. Pricing Strategy Analysis

### Model 1: Per-Lead Pricing

**How it works:** Dealers pay a fixed fee for each consumer lead delivered.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Industry CPL range | $20-$150 (third-party); $283 avg all sources |
| Proposed price point | $35-$75 per lead |
| Revenue predictability | Low -- fluctuates with lead volume |
| Dealer preference | Moderate -- familiar model, but dealers dislike paying for unqualified leads |

**Pros:**
- Low barrier to entry for dealers
- Easy to understand and sell
- Aligns revenue with value delivery

**Cons:**
- Revenue volatility
- Pressure to generate volume over quality
- Dealers may question lead quality (industry pain point)
- No recurring revenue base

### Model 2: Per-Sale (Pay-for-Performance)

**How it works:** Dealers pay only when a lead converts to a sale (TrueCar model).

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Industry pricing | $299/new sale, $399/used sale (TrueCar) |
| Revenue predictability | Very low -- depends on dealer closing ability |
| Dealer preference | High -- zero risk for dealer |

**Pros:**
- Extremely attractive to dealers (pay only for results)
- Aligns incentives perfectly
- Higher per-unit revenue

**Cons:**
- Attribution is extremely difficult (92% of sales are untraceable to a specific lead source)
- Long sales cycles (weeks to months) delay revenue recognition
- Requires deep DMS/CRM integration to verify sales
- Cash flow challenges for a startup
- Dealers can game the system by not reporting sales

### Model 3: SaaS Subscription

**How it works:** Dealers pay a monthly fee for platform access, lead flow, and incentive tools.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Industry range | $450-$10,000/month depending on platform and tier |
| Revenue predictability | High -- recurring monthly revenue |
| Dealer preference | Low initially (commitment required before seeing value) |

**Pros:**
- Predictable recurring revenue (attractive to investors)
- Higher lifetime value per dealer
- Enables investment in product quality over lead volume
- Upsell opportunities through tiered features

**Cons:**
- High barrier to entry for new dealers
- Requires demonstrating value before dealers commit
- Churn risk if lead quality doesn't meet expectations
- Harder to sell without established brand

### Model 4: Hybrid (Recommended)

**How it works:** Low-cost subscription base + per-lead fees, with optional performance bonuses.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Base subscription | $299-$799/month |
| Per-lead fee | $25-$50 per qualified lead |
| Revenue predictability | Moderate-high (subscription base + variable upside) |
| Dealer preference | High -- low commitment entry, pay more as value proves out |

**Pros:**
- Recurring base covers infrastructure costs
- Per-lead component aligns incentives
- Scalable -- dealers can start small and grow
- Blended revenue model reduces risk
- Enables freemium/trial onboarding

**Cons:**
- More complex pricing to communicate
- Requires balance between base fee and per-lead pricing

## 4. Recommended Pricing Model

### Tiered Hybrid Model

#### Tier 1: Starter -- $299/month + $40/lead
- Up to 25 leads/month included in base
- Incentive widget for dealer website
- Basic analytics dashboard
- Email lead delivery
- **Target:** Independent and small franchise dealers

#### Tier 2: Growth -- $599/month + $35/lead
- Up to 50 leads/month included in base
- Advanced incentive calculator widget (co-branded)
- CRM integration (VinSolutions, Elead, DriveCentric)
- Lead scoring and prioritization
- Monthly incentive trend reports
- **Target:** Mid-size franchise dealers

#### Tier 3: Enterprise -- $999/month + $30/lead
- Up to 100 leads/month included in base
- White-label incentive tools
- Multi-rooftop management
- Dedicated account manager
- Custom API integrations
- Priority lead routing
- **Target:** Large dealer groups

#### Consumer-Side Revenue (Phase 2, Year 2+)
- **Affiliate commissions:** Earn from lender referrals, insurance quotes, charger equipment purchases
- **Manufacturer partnerships:** OEMs pay for featured placement of incentive-eligible vehicles
- Estimated additional $5-$15 per consumer interaction

### Pricing Rationale

| Factor | Our Position |
|--------|-------------|
| Industry avg CPL (all sources) | $283 |
| Third-party platform CPL | $20-$150 |
| Our effective CPL at Starter tier | $52 (base amortized + per-lead) |
| Our effective CPL at Enterprise tier | $40 (base amortized + per-lead) |
| TrueCar per-sale fee | $299-$399 |
| Our implied cost-per-sale at 15% close rate | $267-$347 |

Our pricing is competitive with third-party platforms while delivering higher-quality, incentive-enriched leads that should convert at 2-3x industry average rates.

## 6. TAM / SAM / SOM Analysis

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)

**US dealer advertising + lead generation spend:**
- 18,393 franchised dealership rooftops (Urban Science, 2024)
- ~22,000 total new + used-only dealerships actively advertising
- Average advertising spend: $543,539/year (NADA 2024)
- Third-party lead spend: $50,000-$200,000/year per dealer
- Total US dealer advertising market: **$9.22 billion** (2024)
- Lead generation subset (third-party + digital lead gen): **~$3.5 billion**

**TAM = $3.5 billion** (US automotive lead generation services)

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)

Our SAM focuses on dealers who:
- Sell EVs, PHEVs, or vehicles with significant manufacturer incentives (est. 70% of franchised dealers)
- Are in states with active state incentive programs (est. 60% of dealers)
- Currently use third-party lead providers (est. 80% of franchise dealers)

**Calculation:**
- 18,393 franchised rooftops x 70% EV/incentive-relevant x 60% incentive-active states x 80% third-party lead users
- = ~6,180 target dealers
- Average annual lead gen spend per dealer: ~$120,000
- **SAM = $742 million**

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)

**Year 1 SOM:**
- Target: 75 dealer accounts

- **Year 1 SOM = $1.08 million**

**Year 5 SOM (projection):**

## 11. Key Assumptions & Risks

### Key Assumptions

| Assumption | Basis |
|-----------|-------|
| Dealers pay $499/mo avg subscription | Below industry avg ($500-$10,000/mo for competitors) |
| $40 avg per-lead fee | Below industry avg CPL of $283; competitive with $20-$150 range |
| 70 leads/dealer/month at steady state | Conservative vs. AutoTrader/CarGurus volume |
| 10% monthly dealer churn (Year 1) | Industry avg for new platforms; improving to 5% by Year 3 |
| Incentive-aware leads convert at 12-15% | 2-3x the 5-8% industry average for third-party leads |
| Blended internal cost per lead of $17.50 | Decreasing over time as organic traffic grows |
| Consumer-side revenue from Year 2 | Based on affiliate/OEM partnership models proven by TrueCar, Edmunds |

### Risk Matrix

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Incentive programs reduced/eliminated** | Medium | High | Diversify to manufacturer incentives (always exist); expand to financing optimization |
| **Competitor copies incentive features** | High | Medium | First-mover advantage; depth of data is hard to replicate quickly; patents on methodology |
| **Dealer adoption slower than projected** | Medium | High | Reduce subscription pricing; offer extended free trials; focus on per-lead model initially |
| **Lead quality doesn't meet expectations** | Low | High | Continuous quality scoring; money-back guarantee on fraudulent leads; exclusive lead distribution |
| **Regulatory changes (TCPA, privacy)** | Medium | Medium | Privacy-by-design architecture; consent management built in; legal counsel on retainer |
| **CAC higher than projected** | Medium | Medium | Leverage dealer association partnerships; OEM co-op programs; referral incentives |
| **Key person risk** | Medium | Medium | Document processes early; hire second technical lead by Month 6 |
| **Recession / auto market downturn** | Low-Medium | High | Incentive-aware leads become MORE valuable during downturns (savings matter more) |

### Counter-Cyclical Advantage

Notably, this business has a **natural hedge against economic downturns**:
- When the economy slows, consumers become more price-sensitive and incentive-seeking
- Manufacturers increase incentive spending to move inventory (2025 avg: 7.3% of ATP)
- Dealers become more desperate for qualified leads as walk-in traffic declines
- The value proposition of "find every dollar of savings" strengthens in tough times
