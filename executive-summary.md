# IncentiveDrive: Executive Summary

**Incentive-Aware Automotive Lead Generation Platform**

*March 2026 | Confidential*

---

## The Opportunity

A massive gap exists in the $9.2 billion U.S. automotive dealer advertising market: **no major lead generation platform centers its value proposition around incentive intelligence**.

This is remarkable because:

- Manufacturer incentives averaged **18% of average transaction price** ($10,473/vehicle) for EVs in December 2025
- Federal EV tax credits expired September 30, 2025, creating enormous consumer confusion about remaining state, utility, and manufacturer incentives
- Incentives from all sources (state + manufacturer + utility + affinity) can stack to **$15,000-$33,000+** on qualifying vehicles
- **20+ state programs** each have different rules, income limits, funding status, and deadlines
- **43% of consumers would switch brands for a lower price**, and **62% feel vehicle ownership is too costly** -- savings-motivated buyers are a large and growing segment
- Yet no one -- not AutoTrader, CarGurus, Cars.com, TrueCar, or Edmunds -- builds the consumer experience around "what's the best deal I can get?"

Consumers spend **14+ hours researching online** before purchase, with **61% of traffic from mobile devices**. They demand price transparency (65% call it the most important factor) and trustworthy data sources -- yet no single platform aggregates all incentive types into a unified, personalized savings view.

---

## The Problem We Solve

### For Consumers
Incentive complexity is overwhelming. A buyer in Colorado could stack state credits ($3,250), manufacturer rebates ($2,000-$12,750), utility rebates ($50-$4,200), affinity discounts ($500-$1,500), and the remaining 30C charger credit ($1,000) -- but no single resource personalizes this. Consumers leave thousands of dollars on the table because they don't know what they qualify for.

### For Dealers
Dealers spend **$543,539/year** on advertising with an industry-average CPL of **$283**. They face:
- **30% of third-party leads are fraudulent** (bot submissions, recycled leads)
- **70%+ of leads never respond** to any outreach
- **92% of sales are untraceable** to a specific lead source
- Shared leads create price wars that destroy margins
- No lead provider qualifies buyers by incentive eligibility

---

## The Solution

**IncentiveDrive** is an incentive-aware lead generation platform that:

1. **Consumer side:** Offers a personalized incentive calculator ("What savings do you qualify for?") that captures ZIP code, income range, vehicle interest, and affinity status -- generating detailed savings reports across all incentive layers
2. **Dealer side:** Delivers pre-qualified, incentive-enriched leads with specific dollar amounts and qualifying programs, enabling faster deal structuring and higher close rates
3. **Data moat:** Maintains a continuously updated database of 200+ incentive programs (federal, state, local, manufacturer, utility, affinity) with stacking optimization

### Why Incentive-Aware Leads Are Worth More

| Lead Type | Typical CPL | Expected Close Rate |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| Generic third-party lead | $20-$50 | 5-8% |
| Incentive-aware lead | $50-$75 | 10-12% |
| Incentive-qualified + exclusive | $150-$300 | 15-20% |

A lead that arrives at a dealer with "this consumer qualifies for $12,000 in combined incentives on these 3 vehicles" is fundamentally more valuable than a generic contact request.

## Technical Architecture

### Core System

- **Consumer app:** Next.js (SSR for SEO -- the primary acquisition channel)
- **Backend:** Python/FastAPI (incentive matching engine, lead scoring)
- **Database:** PostgreSQL (complex relational incentive data with stacking rules)
- **Infrastructure:** AWS (ECS Fargate/App Runner), ~$230/mo at MVP

### Key Architectural Decisions

1. **ADF XML lead delivery first** -- covers 95% of dealer CRMs at zero integration cost, before investing in direct CRM APIs
2. **Incentive matching engine as core IP** -- no existing API covers all incentive types with stacking optimization; this is the moat
3. **SEO-first acquisition** -- state-specific incentive pages drive organic traffic at the lowest CPL ($15-40 vs $25-45 paid)
4. **Build to California compliance standard** -- satisfies most state privacy laws with one implementation

### MVP Scope (Months 1-3)

**Already built (consumer-facing MVP):**
- ZIP code auto-detection via IP geolocation (auto-searches on page load)
- Cash / Finance / Lease purchase type tabs on homepage search and results page
- Source attribution links on every incentive ("See more info" links to official source URL)
- Incentive stacking calculator with mutual exclusion rules
- Progressive eligibility questionnaire (ZIP -> vehicle -> income/tax -> affiliations)
- Value-first lead capture ("Connect with a Dealer" shown after full results)
- Mobile-responsive design

**Remaining MVP work:**
- Incentive data coverage for top 15 states + OEM incentives via MarketCheck API
- Lead capture with TCPA-compliant consent + TrustedForm verification
- Rule-based lead scoring and ADF XML delivery to dealers
- Basic dealer portal
- **Team:** 2-3 engineers, 1 incentive data analyst, 1 contract designer
- **Infrastructure cost:** ~$1,200/mo (excluding salaries)

## Consumer UX Strategy

Our consumer experience is designed around findings from UX research on savings-motivated car buyers (see [ux-research-brief.md](ux-research-brief.md)).

### Research-Backed Design Principles

1. **ZIP code as primary entry point.** Every successful automotive site uses ZIP for personalization. We auto-detect via IP geolocation and immediately show localized results -- reducing friction to first meaningful interaction.

2. **Purchase type comparison.** Cash, finance, and lease deals carry different incentives. Our tab-based UI lets users compare stacked savings across all three -- a capability no competitor offers with full incentive stacking.

3. **Source attribution builds trust.** 65% of buyers cite price transparency as the most important factor. Every incentive on our platform links to its official source (state program, manufacturer announcement), addressing the #1 trust concern for deal-skeptical users.

4. **Progressive disclosure over gated forms.** Instead of asking for all info upfront, we progressively collect ZIP -> vehicle -> income/tax -> affiliations, updating the savings total at each step. Lead capture ("Connect with a Dealer") appears only after users see their full personalized results -- a value-first approach that outperforms traditional "Submit" CTAs.

5. **Mobile-first.** With 61% of automotive traffic from mobile and 70%+ of shoppers using smartphones as their primary research device, our responsive design prioritizes thumb-friendly navigation, fast load times, and collapsible incentive cards.

### Competitive Differentiation

| Capability | Competitors (TrueCar, Edmunds, KBB, CarsDirect) | IncentiveDrive |
|---|---|---|
| Incentive stacking calculator | None offer this | Built and live |
| Personalized eligibility | ZIP code only | ZIP + income + status + utility |
| Source attribution | Inconsistent | Every incentive linked to source |
| Purchase type comparison with stacking | Partial (no stacking) | Full comparison with stacked incentives |
| Utility rebates | None cover this | Included in stacking |

## Regulatory & Compliance

### Critical Risks (Ranked)

| Risk | Severity | Key Requirement |
|------|----------|----------------|
| **TCPA violations** | Critical | $500-$1,500 per call/text; 507 class actions in Q1 2025 (112% YoY increase); avg settlement $6.6M |
| **State privacy laws** | High | 20+ states with active laws; build to CA standard ($7,500/violation intentional) |
| **FTC Safeguards Rule** | High | Likely applies as "finder"; encryption, MFA, penetration testing required; $11K/day penalty |
| **CAN-SPAM** | High | $53,088 per email violation |
| **Do Not Call** | High | $51,744 per call; scrub every 31 days |
| **Data broker registration** | Medium | CA ($6K/yr), TX ($300 + $10K bond), VT ($100) |

### Compliance Strategy

- **Single-seller consent** even though one-to-one rule was vacated (best practice, reduces litigation risk)
- **TrustedForm certificates** for independent consent verification on every lead
- **Jornaya/LeadiD** for fraud detection (addresses the 30% fraudulent lead problem)
- **DNC scrubbing** automated every 31 days against federal and state registries
- **Privacy-by-design:** Build to California CCPA/CPRA standard universally
- **30+ item launch checklist** documented covering legal, consent, DNC, email, data security, and operations

| Item | Cost |

## Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Incentive programs reduced/eliminated | Diversify to manufacturer incentives (always exist); expand to financing optimization |
| Competitor copies incentive features | First-mover data moat; incentive aggregation + stacking is genuinely hard to replicate |
| Dealer adoption slower than projected | Extend free trials; default to per-lead model (no commitment) |
| Lead quality doesn't meet expectations | Money-back guarantee on fraudulent leads; max 3 dealers per lead |
| Regulatory changes | Privacy-by-design; TCPA counsel on retainer; consent infrastructure from day one |
